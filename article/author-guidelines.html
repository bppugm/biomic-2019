Author guidelines

  1. Papers submitted must be original work and should not have been previously published or presented at another meeting.
  2. Papers should be written in English.
  3. Abstract should be no more than 200 words (excluding abstract title, authors, and affiliations).
  4. Paper size as the journal template in PDF format.
  5. For both primary and co-authors, credentials along with their full names and email addresses must be included. If co-authors are from different institutions, the secondary institution(s) and the institution investigators are associate with must be listed.
  6. Papers topic should conform to the scope of the conference/symposium.
  7. Please use one of the proceeding/journal templates whose scopes correspond your topic. You can download provided template below:
Bioinformatics and Biological Data Mining
  1. Paper size are A4 or Letter
  2. Fonts : Times New Roman
  3. Size of every part of the manuscript is different :
      4. [a] Title : 24
      [b] Author and abstract : 9
      [c] Text : 10
  4. Margins, column widths, line spacing, and type styles are built-in; examples of the type styles are provided throughout this document and are identified in italic type, within parentheses, following the example.
  5. Margins :
      6. [a] Top : 1,9 cm
      [b] Left : 1,6 cm
      [c] Bottom : 2,54 cm
      [d] Right : 1,6 cm
      [e]Gutter position : Left
  6. Pages of main paper (include of abstract),
      7. [a] Min : 4 pages
      [b] Max : 6 pages
      [c] Another pages was category as additional paper
  7. Graphics : 300 dpi

Biomedical Sciences
  1. All paper submitted must be written in British English language
  2. The paper should not exceed 6000-word count, 4-7 figures/table and 50 references
  3. Abstract : the word count should not exceed 250 words (original paper).
  4. Fonts : Times New Roman, size 12 with double-line spacing.
  5. Margins for left, right, top and bottom should be 2.54 cm (1 inch).
  6. Do not use boldface for emphasis within text.
  7. Numbers one to ten are written out in words unless they are used as a unit of measurement, except in figures and tables.
  8. Use single hard-returns to separate paragraphs.
  9. Do not use tabs or indents to start a paragraph.
  10. You can use page numbering.
  11. Images as TIFF/JPEG files should be submitted with a minimum resolution of 300 dpi.
  12. Each Figure and table must be submitted separately without figure legend and title. (Authors are advised to keep backup files of all images).
  13. The figure should cover a minimum of 85-95% of total area of the figure, and the margin area/space should not exceed more than 10%.
  14. Do not use the automated features of your software, such as hyphenation, headers, or footers (especially for references).
  15. The citation and bibliographical style of all reference sources (book, chapter in a book, journal articles and internet) should adhere to the Vancouver citation style.
  16. References in text, table and legends should be numbered in parenthesis [e.g. (1), (1, 4), (1-3), (1, 3-5)] and cited consecutively in the order of appearance in the manuscript.

Biomolecular and Biotechnology
  1. Papers must be written in English.
  2. Paper size A4 with maximum of paper are 12 pages.
  3. Maximum abstract are 200 words.
  4. The first page should contain:
      5. [a] Title-bold, 18 point size.
      [b] Name/s of author/s-bold, 10 point size (Surname or Family name first followed by first name).
      [c] Affiliation/s-address of all the author/s followed by email of corresponding author in 8.5 point size.
      [d] Abstract in italics, 12 point size.
      [e] Keywords- at least 5 keywords in 11 point size.
      [f] Text : Times New Roman font, size - 11 point with single spacing.
  5. Margin 2 cm all sides of the text.
  6. All the text should be single spaced.
  7. Alignment/Paragraph : Left
  8. Main Text-The paper should be divided into the following sections :
    Introduction, Material and Methods, Results and Discussion, Conclusion, Acknowledgement and References
  9. Pages should be properly numbered.
  10. Tables and Figures must be in separate sheet/pages.
  11. Graphic : (max) 72dpi resolution.
  12. References must be serially numbered and should be arranged alphabetically by family name.
  13. References-References should be cited in the text as superscript on persons name1, 2 or at the end of the sentence3.

  1. Either British or American English spelling, not a mixture of both.
  2. Size Paper : A4
  3. Abstract : should consist of a single paragraph of no more than 200 words.
  4. Margin :
      5. [a] Top, Left and Right : 2 cm
      [b] Bottom : 2,5 cm
      [c] 4000–6000 words in length (excluding tables, captions, and references).
  5. Standard 11pt serif font (e.g. Times New Roman) for bosy paper; double-spaced.
  6. Title : use a concise and informative title in sentence case, with a maximum of 16 words.
  7. Affiliaon : provide the full postal address of each author’s affiliation, including the street name and number, city, ZIP code, and country.
  8. Keywords : include a maximum of five keywords or phrases, arranged alphabetically and separated using semicolons (;). Use specific, relevant terms that do not appear in the title, so that the article is easier to find in search engines.
  9. Headings : use no more than three levels of headings (e.g. 2, 2.1, and 2.1.1, but no 2.1.1.1).
  10. Figure size and quality. When preparing your figures, size them to fit in a column width (either 80 mm or 170 mm), with a maximum height of 230 mm. Ensure that images are of sufficiently high resolution to be easily viewable (minimum of 300 dpi). One figure per file.
  11. Image file format (png, jpeg, tiff), vector (eps, svg), or pdf
  12. Graphs. Graphs created with Microsoft Excel should also be sent in their original Excel file. Present graphs in 2D (not 3D), without shadows or other effects, and without gridlines.
  13. Table size. Size tables to fit in a column width (either 80 mm or 170 mm), with a maximum height of 230 mm. Use only horizontal lines for borders.
  14. Captions file (put all captions for figures and tables into one file).
  15. File naming. Name your figure files “Figure” with the figure number; e.g. Figure1.jpeg. Name your tables file “Tables”; e.g. Tables.docx or Tables.xlsx. Tables file (put all tables into one file).
  16. References : for the purposes of efficiency and conciseness, avoid using more than 25 references and less than 10. References file in BibTeX format (.bib).

  4. Use 12-point Times New Roman font
  5. Margin : Normal
  6. Formatted as standard A4 page setup with double-space and 1-inch margin on the left and right sides.
  7. Keywords should be provided no more than 5 words and separated by commas
